## The Core Business Problems Faced by Pakistani Restaurateurs
Operating a restaurant in Pakistan is a complex balancing act. Many owners suffer from significant operational **leakages** that go unnoticed until the end-of-month profit-and-loss statement arrives.

*   **The Inventory Leakage Problem:** One of the most persistent issues is "invisible wastage." Without an AI-driven system, tracking the precise conversion rate from raw ingredients to finished plates is impossible. This leads to stock mismanagement where items expire or are stolen.
*   **The Regulatory Compliance Challenge:** With the FBR, SRB (Sindh Revenue Board), and PRA (Punjab Revenue Authority) increasingly mandating real-time POS integration, restaurateurs face the **problem** of compliance. Non-integrated systems often lead to tax-related penalties and audit nightmares.
*   **The Cash Management Challenge:** Relying heavily on cash creates high-security risks and manual reconciliation errors. When staff handles both food and money, cross-contamination and financial **leakage**—such as under-reporting sales—are significant risks.
*   **The Service Bottleneck Challenge:** In peak hours, manual order taking leads to long queues, which directly results in customer churn. If a customer at a busy Lahore burger joint waits 15 minutes to simply place an order, they are likely to never return.
